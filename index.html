Why This Exists
"Most compliance systems are built for regulators, not operators. They produce reports for someone else to decide. Decision infrastructure is different — the decision is the output, not the input to another meeting."
Operator Mindset

Analysis does not
create outcomes.
Execution does.

The gap between knowing a risk exists and doing something traceable about it — that gap is where most compliance systems fail. Not because of bad data. Because of missing infrastructure.

Infrastructure is the layer that takes a risk signal, resolves it against operational constraints, and produces a decision that can be acted on, audited, and defended in front of a regulator.

That is the only work worth doing.

→
Sanctions matched before settlement occurs. Not flagged for review after the transaction clears — intercepted before execution, when intervention is still possible.
→
Investigation evidence assembled automatically. When a BLOCK fires, the case package already exists. Analysts review structured evidence — not raw data from scratch.
→
Every decision carries its own reasoning. Signal → weight → score → decision → reason. Each step preserved. Each step defensible under audit.
→
Routine decisions removed from analyst workload entirely. Low-risk volume is cleared automatically. Human judgment reserved for cases that genuinely require it.
→
Constraint is the design condition. Built for incomplete data, adversarial signals, time pressure — not ideal laboratory inputs.

02

  • Wallet submits 5 transactions in 3 minutes
  • Each: $8,000–$12,000 — below individual alert thresholds
  • Destinations: multiple different contracts, several unknown
  • Rule-based result: no alerts triggered on any single transaction
  • Pattern consistent with structuring behavior

  • Aggregate risk score: 70 — elevated, not critical
Outcome

  • All 5 transactions in one structured case — no reconstruction needed
  • Pattern escalated for enhanced monitoring on wallet
  • Analyst reviews evidence — does not build it
